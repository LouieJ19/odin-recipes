Crawfish Gumbo

Description

Plump crawfish tails, succulent andouille sausage, and vibrant southern vegetables mixed into a rich and spicy roux-based sauce. Served with fluffy white rice. Serves 8.

Ingredients

  • Crawfish 2lbs
  • Andouille Sausage, Sliced 1lb
  • Celery, Chopped 1 cup
  • Onion, Chopped 1 cup
  • Green Bell Pepper, Chopped 1 cup
  • Okra, Sliced 1 lb
  • Garlic, minced 8 cloves
  • Butter, 1 stick
  • Flour, 1 cup
  • Chicken Stock, 1 cup
  • Crushed Tomatoes 24oz
  • Creole Seasoning, 4 tablespoons
  • Cooked White Rice, 2 cups

Steps

  1. Start by creating a roux by heating a small pan over a low flame and melting the butter. Mix flour into butter as it melts. Keep mixture on low until it is golden brown or to your likeing.
  2. Boil Crawfish for 10 minutes. Remove Crawfish meat from shells and set aside.
  3. Sautee sausages in olive oil in your stock pot for 5 min on med-high heat. Set aside when lightly browned.
  4. Add celery, onion, and green pepper to stock pot. Sautee in olive oil until soft and translucent, about 10 min.
  5. Add garlic to stock pot, sautee until fragrent, about 2 min.
  6. Add chicken stock, crushed tomatoes, creole seasoning and roux. Stir together until a consistant smooth sauce is formed.
  7. Stir in okra, until sauce is thickened.
  8. Throw the crawfish and sausage meat into the stock pot and stir until everything is well mixed.
  9. Serve in a bowl with a scoop of white rice in the middle of the gumbo.
