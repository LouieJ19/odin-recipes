Smoked Beef Brisket

Description

Texas-style beef brisket that is slow-cooked for 20 hours and quite literally melts in your mouth. Serves 20.

Ingredients

  • Flat-cut Beef Brisket, 10lbs
  • Kosher salt, 1 cup
  • Black peppercorns, crushed 1 cup
  • Hickory wood chips

Steps

  1. Generously rub salt and pepper over outside of brisket, completely covering it. Cover brisket in foil and store in refrigerator overnight to dry brine.
  2. Remove brisket at least 1 hour before cooking to allow it to get towards room temperature.
  3. Place brisket in electric smoker and add wood chips to tray. Set smoker for 225 degrees.
  4. Cook brisket at this temperature for 8 hours. Replace wood chips as needed.
  5. After 8 hours of smoking, reduce smoker temperature down to 160. Cook for another 12 hours.
  6. Remove brisket and let it rest for 10-30 minutes.
  7. Slice brisket against the grain in long strips.
