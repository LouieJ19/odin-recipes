Carbonara

Description

Linguine tossed in a delicate parmesean sauce with crispy bits of pancetta, seasoned with cracked black pepper. Serves 2.

  • Pancetta, chopped 8oz
  • Linguine Pasta, 1lb
  • Parmesenn cheese, grated 4 oz
  • 1 egg
  • Black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Start by boiling a pot of water for the pasta, add salt to the water so that is as salty as the sea. Time pasta to finish with the pancetta below.
  2. Next, heat a cast iron pan on medium high heat so the surface temperature is between 320-370 degrees.
  3. Sautee pancetta until crispy, but not burnt, bout 10-15 minutes. Turn heat off but leave pan in its place on the stove.
  4. Beat the egg and parmesean cheese with black pepper until it forms a semi-thick and grainily-smooth sauce.
  5. When pasta is done, drain the water and immediately throw the noodles into the cast iron pan with the pancetta bits.
  6. Quickly add the egg mixture to the pasta and toss it until evenly coated.
  7. Twirl enough pasta to cover a craving for and serve nest of Carbonara pasta as a side dish or entree.
